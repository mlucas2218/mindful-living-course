Presentation Guidelines

During the semester, each student will give a short presentation about a topic that they have researched beyond what was covered in class. This provides the opportunity for students to connect course material to their own lives and to produce a "finished product" about what they have learned.

Presentations will be at the beginning of each class, should last roughly 3-5 minutes, and can be done individually or in pairs. The presentation should include some sort of visual component, such as a Powerpoint presentation, poster, short video, or other creative means.

This is your opportunity to explore any relevant topic that uniquely interests or excites you! Some ideas are:

  • Contrast independent studies about the toxicity of plastic, a food additive, or a chemical in a pharmaceutical drug with industry studies about its safety (or visa versa)
  • Look into where the meat/milk/fruit in your servery comes from
  • Research a disease or health condition that may run in your family and naturalistic approaches to cure or prevent it
  • Interview a vendor at the Rice Farmer's Market about how they grow their food and the benefits or drawbacks of small-scale farming as opposed to large-scale industrial food production
  • Investigate how your servery could provide more nutritious, fresh, or environmentally friendly breakfast options to students
  • Discuss the impact of a common personal care product (ex. conventional laundry detergent, fluoride toothpaste, sunscreen containing parabens) on individual/environmental health and potential alternatives to that product

If you don't already have a specific topic in mind, consider relating your presentation to the previous week's topic so that it serves as an expansion on what we discussed the previous class. Ultimately, though, your topic is up to you!
